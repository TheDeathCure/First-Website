Flowers of Japan

Japanese flowers are deeply rooted in culture, often representing seasons, emotions, or virtues through hanakotoba Iconic blooms include sakura (cherry blossoms) for renewal, kiku (chrysanthemum) for the imperial family, and tsubaki (camellia) for admiration. Other significant flowers are hydrangeas, irises, and red spider lilies, which are celebrated in festivals throughout the year. expresses emotions and messages through specific blooms.
